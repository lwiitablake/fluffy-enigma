Printable Decision Cards

Print nine cards per page for workshops, consultations, or faculty development.

Use your browser’s print option and choose Save as PDF or print directly.

Recommended print settings: Letter paper, portrait, default margins, and background graphics on.

Print Instructions

  1. Press Ctrl + P on Windows or Cmd + P on Mac.
  2. Choose Save as PDF or select your printer.
  3. Turn on Background graphics if your browser offers that option.
  4. Print on cardstock if you want to cut these into physical cards.

Clarifying Instructions

AI can help with: simplifying directions, organizing steps, and creating student-friendly wording.

Human review: confirm the instructions match the assignment, grading expectations, due dates, and Canvas setup.

Red flag: AI may invent steps, requirements, or submission details.

Accessibility Review

AI can help with: identifying possible barriers, suggesting alt text drafts, and checking for plain language.

Human review: verify headings, links, captions, tables, contrast, and actual file accessibility.

Red flag: AI may say something is accessible without truly checking it.

Creating Examples

AI can help with: drafting sample scenarios, practice questions, and discipline-specific examples.

Human review: check accuracy, assumptions, reading level, relevance, and diverse student experiences.

Red flag: AI may default to narrow, stereotyped, or generic examples.

OER/ZTC Materials

AI can help with: summarizing open materials, drafting reading guides, and aligning resources to outcomes.

Human review: verify licensing, attribution, source accuracy, and student usability.

Red flag: AI may misstate license terms or invent citations.

Rubrics and Feedback

AI can help with: drafting rubric language, feedback stems, and revision-focused comments.

Human review: ensure criteria match outcomes and support learning without punishing unclear expectations.

Red flag: AI may create vague, punitive, or misaligned criteria.

Student-Facing AI Use

AI can help with: brainstorming, practice, revision, and reflection when expectations are clear.

Human review: clarify allowed uses, disclosure expectations, and what learning belongs to students.

Red flag: unclear policies can create inequity, confusion, or accidental misconduct.

Prompt Planning

AI can help with: turning rough goals into structured prompts with context and constraints.

Human review: confirm the prompt protects privacy and asks for usable, reviewable output.

Red flag: a vague prompt can produce confident but unusable results.

Privacy Check

AI can help with: drafting generic examples or de-identified scenarios.

Human review: remove student names, grades, IDs, accommodations, and private course information.

Red flag: never paste identifiable student information into public AI tools.

Alignment Check

AI can help with: comparing draft materials to outcomes, objectives, and assignment purpose.

Human review: decide whether the activity actually supports the intended learning.

Red flag: AI may make something sound aligned when it only matches keywords.
